---
title: The audit problem
description: Why a sampled, slow audit cannot tell you what you need to know.
---

A traditional solvency audit samples a handful of accounts, runs for weeks, and
ships a report that describes a balance sheet which has already moved on. The
people who depend on that report, depositors, regulators, and counterparties,
are asked to trust a snapshot they cannot reproduce and that was stale before it
was printed. The cost is real: the work is expensive, the coverage is partial,
and the gap between "audited" and "true today" is exactly where failures hide.
This post lays out why the status quo leaves that gap open, and what it would
take to close it. Full content lands in a later issue.
