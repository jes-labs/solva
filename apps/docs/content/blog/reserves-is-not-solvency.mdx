---
title: Proof of Reserves is not Proof of Solvency
description: Attesting assets says nothing about what you owe.
---

A proof of reserves shows that an institution holds a pile of assets. It says
nothing about the size of the claims against those assets. An institution can
publish a large, honest reserve figure and still be deep underwater if its
liabilities are larger, and most published reserve attestations never measure
that side of the ledger at all. Solvency is a relation between two numbers,
reserves and liabilities, so a proof that binds only one of them is not a proof
of solvency. This post explains the difference and why Solva commits both sides
in a single proof. Full content lands in a later issue.
